Project: Contract Intelligence - Search Experience Refresh
Quarter: Q3 planning input
Prepared by: Product (Discovery sprint 2)

Business context:
- Product serves legal ops teams managing 5k to 60k active agreements.
- Search is one of the top three workflows by weekly active usage.
- In the last two quarters, search-related complaints increased as account size grew.
- Revenue team flagged search quality as a recurring blocker in enterprise evaluations.

Evidence collected this cycle:
1) Customer interviews (n=11)
- 6 Legal Ops Managers (mid-market to enterprise)
- 3 In-house Counsel
- 2 Procurement Managers
- 8/11 participants reported "low confidence" in current search relevance.
- 7/11 users said they manually open multiple contracts because top results are noisy.
- Time to locate a specific clause ranged from 3 to 18 minutes depending on metadata quality.

2) Support and success data (last 60 days)
- 47 tickets tagged "search relevance" or "cannot find clause".
- 19 tickets required CSM intervention to locate content that users expected to self-serve.
- Top complaint themes:
  - exact phrase search misses obvious results
  - no way to filter by effective date and counterparty in one step
  - results page does not show enough snippet context

3) Sales and solutions feedback
- 4 enterprise deals in late stage asked for "Google-like clause search".
- 2 opportunities listed search quality as "at risk" in MEDDPICC notes.
- Demo teams currently prepare curated saved views to hide search limitations.

4) Product and technical constraints
- Current implementation indexes title, tags, and limited metadata only.
- Clause body text is stored but not indexed for retrieval.
- Role-based permissions are enforced in API responses, not in index layer.
- Engineering estimate:
  - 1 sprint: full-text indexing + baseline ranking
  - +1 sprint: filter chips, result snippets, ranking tuning
  - +1 sprint: permission-safe index strategy and telemetry hardening

5) UX research notes
- Users start with broad terms, then refine quickly by:
  - contract type
  - jurisdiction
  - counterparty
  - effective date / renewal window
- Trust increases when result snippets show the exact matching phrase in context.
- Users prefer "good enough in <2 seconds" for exploration, but want high precision for final answer workflows.

Hypotheses to validate:
- H1: Clause-body indexing + contextual snippets will reduce time-to-answer by at least 30%.
- H2: Multi-filter refinement will reduce irrelevant result clicks by at least 25%.
- H3: Search confidence is a leading indicator for expansion in legal-heavy enterprise accounts.

Open concerns:
- Potential indexing cost increase with clause-body ingestion.
- Permission leakage risk if access controls are not consistently applied in indexing pipeline.
- Need to define a clear relevance quality metric before rollout.
